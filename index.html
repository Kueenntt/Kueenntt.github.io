Music Theory Basics

Here are some fundamental music theory concepts I've learned in class

Rhythm

The pattern of sounds and silences in time. Rhythm creates the foundation for music.

Melody

A sequence of musical notes that are perceived as a single entity. The tune you remember.

Harmony

When multiple notes are played simultaneously to support the melody.

Dynamics

The volume of music - from very soft (piano) to very loud (forte).

Important Music Concepts

Here are some specific music concepts I've been studying

Crescendo

A crescendo is when the music gradually gets louder. It's shown in sheet music with this symbol: <

I've practiced crescendos on both guitar and drums by gradually playing with more intensity.

Decrescendo

A decrescendo (or diminuendo) is when the music gradually gets softer. It's shown with this symbol: >

This is the opposite of a crescendo and creates a calming effect in music.

Time Signature

The time signature tells you how many beats are in each measure. Common ones are 4/4 (four beats) and 3/4 (three beats).

I've been practicing playing in different time signatures on the drums.

Tempo

Tempo is the speed of the music. Italian words like Allegro (fast) or Adagio (slow) are used to describe tempo.

I use a metronome to practice keeping a steady tempo.
